About Me

I started building computers and networks in high school. I studied philosophy as an undergraduate focusing on continential (scorning anything related to analytic). After graduating I co-founded a contemporary art gallery and went on to be the director at some New York galleries.

Around 2017, I decided to pursue a path that involved my love of philosophy and computers and became fascinated with the advances being made in AI, specifically deep learning. I'm passionate about the empiricism of data science and committed to exploring the vast landscape of machine learning and deep learning in all of its complexities.
